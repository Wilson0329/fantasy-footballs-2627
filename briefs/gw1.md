# Fantasy Footballs — 2026/27 Season Opener Podcast Brief

_Source document for the NotebookLM Audio Overview. Two hosts previewing the new season._

## How to present this
- **Keep it to 5 minutes MAXIMUM.** Short, punchy, fast-paced — hit the headlines and move on, no rambling or long tangents.
- **Audience:** the listeners ARE the twelve managers in this league. They know the rules inside out. Speak to insiders, not newcomers.
- **The draft format is BACKGROUND CONTEXT ONLY — do NOT explain it.** Never describe how a draft or snake draft works, never define terms, never say "in a draft league, unlike normal FPL…". Just talk as if everyone already knows. Let it surface only as natural flavour — e.g. "unique squads", "who nailed their draft", "nobody else can touch your players", "he reached for that pick" — never as an explanation.

## Welcome
Welcome back to Fantasy Footballs for the **2026/27 season**. New season, clean slate — everyone back to zero. Open the show by welcoming the group back and setting up a quick look at last season and the big roster news.

## Last season recap (2025/26)
- **Champion: Charles Smith (Return of the Fox)** — ran away with it on **2040 points**, a colossal **246 clear** of second place. Utterly dominant. Is he that good, or was the rest of the group asleep?
- **Runners-up:** Alexander Bodek (Beef Cherki) 2nd on 1794; Sam Haseltine (Soucek Madness) 3rd on 1767.
- **Cup: Dominic Byrne (HC XI II)** lifted the trophy, edging Jason Knightly's Inevitable **47–43** in a tight final. HC XI II beat Soucek Madness in the semis; Inevitable knocked out Beef Cherki.
- **The drop:** Aron Rouse (Gyök, Stock & Barrel) finished rock bottom on 1512 and was **relegated**, opening up a spot.

## Roster changes for 2026/27 (the headline news)
1. **A champion returns — Ant Savides joins the group.** Ant is no makeweight: he's a **former league champion** (he sits in the Hall of Champions). Title-winning pedigree walking straight back in. Does he contend immediately, or is he rusty after time away?
2. **Joe Wilson's great escape.** Joe (Forever20) is, statistically, **the most successful manager in the league's history** — top of the Hall of Champions with four league titles and a cup. Yet he endured a nightmare 2025/26, finishing **11th, inside the relegation places**. Normally that's the drop. But because **only one new player (Ant) entered the group**, only one relegation spot was needed — Aron went down, and **Joe clings on by the skin of his teeth**. The greatest of all time, spared by the numbers.

## Tone — this is a roast, not a broadcast
The two hosts are mates who live to wind each other, and the whole league, up. Go HARD on the banter: sarcastic, savage, take the mick out of the managers the way you'd rip into a friend down the pub. Cheap shots about their fantasy ability, dodgy draft picks and past humiliations are not just allowed, they're the point. Keep it affectionate underneath — these are friends, so it's ruthless but never genuinely nasty (no low blows outside of football). Land the jokes fast, no polite filler.

- **Charles ran away with it — 2040 points, a ludicrous 246 clear.** Either a bona fide genius… or living proof the other eleven haven't got the first clue. Rinse the chasing pack for making him look that good — a title that says more about them than him.
